Founding

Why Pravāh

The case for science-driven river restoration starts with one idea. If the problem is visible on the ground, the response should also be grounded in real field data.

The approach

Pravāh is built around two wings. One wing is technical, focused on research and restoration. The other wing is social, focused on people, action, and long-term support.

That structure matters because river work is not only about measurements. It also depends on public trust, clear communication, and steady involvement from people who care.

What we want to change

The aim is not to make river work look complicated. The aim is to make it useful, consistent, and open enough for more people to help.
